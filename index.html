A Gathering for Families

Raising kids
should feel like magic

Not survival. Not management. Magic.

Tribe Camp is an experiment in family living. A week where parents remember what it feels like to have fun with their kids. Where children make friends across ages and continents. Where we build, create, sing, and discover that we're not meant to do this alone.

The Manifesto

We believe raising children was never meant to be a solo sport. For most of human history, kids grew up in villages ‚Äî surrounded by aunties, uncles, cousins, neighbors, elders. We've lost that. We want to build it back, one gathering at a time.

‚ú¶ ‚ú¶ ‚ú¶
  • Intergenerational wisdom flows both ways. Teenagers mentor toddlers. New parents learn from those who've walked the path. Elders share stories. Everyone has something to teach.
  • Children are included, not segregated. No "kids' clubs" that warehouse children while adults drink elsewhere. We cook together, build together, create together. Kids belong in every space.
  • We leave things better than we found them. This isn't consumption ‚Äî it's co-creation. We build the playground. We plant the garden. We make something that lasts.
  • Joy is the point. Not productivity. Not optimization. We dance in the forest at sunset. We make terrible art and laugh about it. We remember that family life can be fun.
  • The village is global. We bring together families from Singapore to Barcelona, from Warsaw to wherever you're reading this. Our children will inherit a connected world ‚Äî let's help them thrive in it.
  • We're building the future by living it now. This isn't just a vacation. It's a prototype of the world we want our grandchildren to grow up in. More connection. More wonder. More tribe.

The Bigger Dream

This first gathering is just the beginning. We imagine Tribe Camp becoming a global movement ‚Äî festivals where intergenerational wisdom flows freely, where young people considering family life can witness what's possible, and where we collectively build the world we want to inherit.

We're starting small. One week. A handful of families. A villa near Barcelona. But the vision is vast: a network of families who support each other across borders, who show up for each other's milestones, whose children grow up knowing they have a tribe scattered across the globe.

"The nuclear family was an experiment. It hasn't worked out that well for most of us. Let's try something older, something more human: the tribe."

This is an invitation to be part of the founding story. The families who join us this first year won't just be attendees ‚Äî they'll be co-creators, helping shape what Tribe Camp becomes for years to come.
