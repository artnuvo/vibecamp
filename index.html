Not survival mode. Not just getting by. Actual magic.

I'm organizing a week for families who are tired of doing this alone. We'll build stuff, make music, let kids roam, cook together, and remember what it feels like to actually enjoy our children. It's an experiment. I don't have all the answers. But I think it might be great.

Why I'm Doing This

For most of human history, kids grew up surrounded by aunties, uncles, cousins, neighbors. Someone always had an eye on them. Someone was always making soup. We've lost that, and I think we're lonelier for it.

‚ú¶ ‚ú¶ ‚ú¶
  • Teenagers teach toddlers. And new parents learn from those who've been through it. Everyone has something to share, even if it's just "I survived three kids under five and here's what helped."
  • Kids are everywhere. No separate kids' club where they're warehoused so adults can drink in peace. We cook together. Build together. They belong in every space, even the messy ones.
  • Leave something behind. We'll build a treehouse or a playground. Plant something. Make the place better than we found it, not just take nice photos and leave.
  • Joy is allowed. Dance in the forest at sunset. Make terrible art. Sing badly. Family life doesn't have to be a slog.
  • The village can be global. I'm imagining families from different countries, different backgrounds, all muddling through parenthood together. Our kids will inherit a connected world. Let's show them how to thrive in it.

The Bigger Picture (Maybe)

If this works ‚Äî if this week is even half as good as I'm hoping ‚Äî maybe we'll do it again. Maybe other people will do their own versions. Maybe we'll figure out how to support each other across borders, show up for birthdays, become the kind of tribe our kids grow up knowing they have.

Or maybe it's just one good week with good people. That would be enough.

The nuclear family was an experiment. It hasn't worked out that well for most of us. Let's try something older.

This first year, the families who come won't just be guests. You'll be co-creators, figuring out what this thing is alongside me. Fair warning: it's going to be messy. Also fair warning: it might be wonderful.
